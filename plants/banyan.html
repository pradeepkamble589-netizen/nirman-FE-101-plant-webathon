Banyan Tree 🌳

Scientific Name

Ficus benghalensis

Family

Moraceae

Description

The banyan tree is a large, iconic tree native to India. It is known for its aerial roots and wide canopy, often called a “walking tree” because its branches can form new trunks.

Habitat

Banyan trees grow in tropical and subtropical climates. They prefer well-drained soil and plenty of sunlight.

Benefits

  • Provides shade and oxygen
  • Medicinal properties in leaves, bark, and roots
  • Supports birds and insects
  • Prevents soil erosion

Uses

  • Used in traditional medicine
  • Leaves for rituals and cultural purposes
  • Shade tree in parks and roadsides

Environmental Importance

  • Provides habitat for wildlife
  • Maintains ecological balance
  • Improves soil quality
