Tulsi 🌿

Scientific Name

Ocimum tenuiflorum

Family

Lamiaceae

Description

Tulsi, also called Holy Basil, is a sacred plant in India. It is famous for its medicinal and spiritual significance. Tulsi is a small, aromatic, perennial shrub.

Habitat

Tulsi grows well in warm climates and sunlight. It is widely cultivated in India, Southeast Asia, and tropical regions.

Benefits

  • Boosts immunity
  • Helps in respiratory health
  • Anti-stress and anti-inflammatory properties
  • Supports digestion

Uses

  • Leaves used in herbal teas and medicines
  • Used in religious rituals
  • Essential oils for skincare and health remedies

Environmental Importance

  • Purifies air
  • Acts as a natural insect repellent
  • Supports biodiversity by attracting pollinators
