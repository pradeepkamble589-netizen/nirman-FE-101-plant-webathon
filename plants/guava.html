Guava Tree 🍈

Scientific Name

Psidium guajava

Family

Myrtaceae

Description

Guava is a tropical fruit tree with sweet and nutritious fruits. Its leaves, bark, and fruits are used in traditional medicine.

Habitat

Grows in tropical and subtropical regions. Prefers well-drained soil and sunny conditions.

Benefits

  • Rich in Vitamin C and fiber
  • Supports digestive health
  • Helps in immunity and blood sugar control
  • Antioxidant properties

Uses

  • Fruits eaten fresh or in jams and juices
  • Leaves used for medicinal tea
  • Wood used for small tools and fuel

Environmental Importance

  • Provides shade and oxygen
  • Supports pollinators like bees and butterflies
  • Prevents soil erosion
